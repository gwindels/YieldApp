How it works

Built to make training simple.

01
You answer a few questions
Yield asks about your goals, your schedule, and about your gym. That's all it needs. From that conversation, it builds a program just for you.
02
One thing at a time
When you're at the gym, Yield shows you exactly what to do next. You do the set, log it, and move on. There's nothing to figure out.
03
Ask anything, mid-session
During an exercise, you can ask Yield questions. It can explain a movement, offer a modification, or pull up a reference image when a description isn't enough.
04
The program refines itself
Between sessions, Yield looks at how you rated each exercise and anything you noted. It uses that to make small adjustments to weight, volume, and pacing.

What is Perceived Exertion?

Adaptive load

The program adjusts based on what you log.

RPE stands for Rate of Perceived Exertion. It's a simple 1–10 scale for rating how hard a set was. 10 means you couldn't have done another rep, 5 means you had plenty left in the tank.

Yield uses those ratings to adjust weights in upcoming sessions. Lighter after a hard week, heavier after an easy one.

Philosophy

Why it's built the way it is.

Yield is built to ease your mind so you focus on your session.

01
One thing at a time
While working out, you only see what's needed for the current set. There's no dashboard, no feed, and nothing in the background.
02
Goals with dates
Your protocol isn't a list of workouts. It has objectives you're trying to achieve, detailed records, and dates you're working toward.
03
No games
Yield doesn't send you streaks or badges. It keeps an accurate log of what you did, and lets the results speak for themselves.
04
Gets better over time
The more sessions you log, the more precisely it calibrates to you. You get exactly what you need and nothing more.
05
Works for any level
Yield works just as well whether you're new to the gym, chasing a personal record, or working on nailing your Iron X.
06
No account required
Everything is stored on your phone. Nothing you do in Yield is tracked or logged on a server.
